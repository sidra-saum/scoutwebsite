Our History

The Beginning of Scouting

The Scout Movement began in 1907 when Robert Baden-Powell, a Lieutenant General in the British Army, held the first Scout camp on Brownsea Island in England. His book "Scouting for Boys" became the manual for the movement that quickly spread worldwide.

Our Local Troop's Founding

Our Scout Troop was established in 1982 as part of Madhupur Shahid Smrity Higher Scondary School's extracurricular program. Founded by Principal William Thompson and Scoutmaster James Wilson, we began with just 12 boys meeting in the school basement.

Over the decades, we've grown into one of the region's most active troops, with over 200 scouts earning Eagle rank and thousands more developing lifelong skills through our program.

Our School Partnership

Madhupur Shahid Smrity Higher Scondary School and Scouting

Since our founding, we've maintained a strong partnership with Madhupur Shahid Smrity Higher Scondary School, one of the region's premier educational institutions established in 1925. The school provides our meeting spaces and supports our activities as part of its commitment to holistic student development.

Shared Values

Both Madhupur Shahid Smrity Higher Scondary School and our Scout Group believe in:

  • Character development through practical experience
  • Leadership through service
  • Lifelong learning and growth
  • Respect for nature and community
